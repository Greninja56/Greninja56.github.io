Reflection Paragraph

This design system reflects me and culture through specific choices in color, typography, and layout. The blues and greens of my color palette convey energy, calmness, and creativity, which are pretty close to me personality, while the Comic Neue font for headings adds a different feel, working with the cleaner Inter font for body text to ensure readability and balance. Visual elements such as the split-color background and consistent spacing feel most authentic to me because they speak personality while maintaining order and structure, making the site visually engaging without feeling chaotic. Accessibility considerations influenced my design decisions, as I have to ensure enough contrast between text and background, readable typography, and visible focus states for interactive components, all while keeping my creative expression. Balancing accessibility with personal style presented challenges, particularly in maintaining readability for playful fonts over vibrant backgrounds. Throughout the design process, I was kinda surprised by how small adjustments to spacing, margins, and typography affected the overall feel of the layout, sticking in the importance of a consistent spacing system and scale in creating a unified visual hierarchy. Adding this system to my portfolio project will allow me to not have a boring white page and keep a consistent and good looking appearance across pages while integrating silly elements that reflect my identity. Looking forward, I could expand the system for different contexts by introducing more color shades, component variations, and responsive spacing rules. The typography and spacing aspects of my system feel highly changeable, allowing for adaptation and scaling, whereas the color palette is slightly less flexible due to its strong connection to my personal identity and the need to maintain accessibility standards, which will continue to guide future projects and help refine some of the design system.
