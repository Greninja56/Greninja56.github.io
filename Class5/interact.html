Reflection

The expandable card shows how small design choices affect user agency. Flexbox creates a clean layout that adapts to different screens, giving a consistent experience. By hiding the details until the button is clicked, people gain control over how much information they see. This helps people who want quick summaries or less words, but it can be not as good for those who need full context or who may not notice the hidden content. Even a simple click requirement changes who finds the interface easy versus who finds it harder. On a bigger scale, hiding and revealing information shows power in digital design. Designers decide what is shown first and what is not, deciding what feels important. In certain projects, this choice determines what is shown overall and what may not be considered key details. While not shown content can reduce overload, it can also be hard to access soemtimes. These small things grow up to influence inclusion and trust in the digital world.
